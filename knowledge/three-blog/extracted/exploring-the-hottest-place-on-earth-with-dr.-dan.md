In the third episode of “Explore with Dr. Dan”, we journey to a place that many would consider inhospitable—the Mojave Desert, home to Death Valley, the hottest place on earth. Temperatures here have reached a scorching 134 degrees Fahrenheit. But what could possibly drive someone to explore this blistering landscape? The answer lies in the hidden treasures of this harsh environment: plants that not only survive but thrive, producing some of the most potent bioactive compounds found in nature.

Dr. Dan Gubler, THREE’s Chief Scientific Officer, takes us on a riveting expedition to discover these incredible plants, revealing their potential to support human health in remarkable ways.













 -












Exploring the Mojave Desert: Nature's Hidden Gems

The Mojave Desert is an extreme environment, a place where only the toughest plants can survive. But in their struggle for survival, these plants have developed an arsenal of powerful molecules that could have profound benefits for human health. The intense conditions force the plants to produce bioactive compounds in higher concentrations, making them a goldmine for researchers like Dr. Dan.












 "These plants haven't just learned how to survive; they've learned how to thrive, producing molecules that can support human health in ways we're only beginning to understand."





















 -












Discovering Hidden Treasures

During this episode, Dr. Dan uncovers several fascinating plants, each with its own unique potential:

Desert Trumpet Plant: Found only in the Mojave Desert, this plant is notable for its hollow sacs. Dr. Dan suspects that these sacs contain molecules that could support gut health—a discovery that could lead to new advancements in digestive wellness.

Apricot Malo: This rare plant might hold the key to better hair health. As Dr. Dan reveals, early studies suggest that the apricot malo contains molecules that could be beneficial for promoting healthy hair, a significant need in today’s world.

Desert Thistle: Already a star ingredient in THREE’s Purifí product, desert thistle is packed with silymarin, known for supporting intestinal wellness. But Dr. Dan isn’t stopping there—he’s on the hunt for even more powerful compounds within this hardy plant.

Ephedrine Neis: This plant was traditionally used by Native Americans and early settlers to create a tea that provided natural energy. The vibrant red color inside the stems hints at the bioactive goodies hidden within, and Dr. Dan is eager to unlock their potential for a future THREE product.

Joshua Tree: Known as the king of the Mojave Desert, the Joshua Tree is more than just a symbol of resilience—it’s a powerhouse of saponins, compounds that enhance the absorption of other nutrients. Dr. Dan is excited to see what new molecules this iconic tree might reveal.

The Grand Finale: Desert Plantain

The pinnacle of this expedition is the discovery of the desert plantain—a plant that has adapted to the extreme conditions of Death Valley in extraordinary ways. This plantain is rich in phytonutrients and possesses a unique composition that hasn’t been seen before. Dr. Dan is particularly excited about its potential to support skin health, and he hints that this discovery will play a pivotal role in a new THREE product that promises to revolutionize the industry.
