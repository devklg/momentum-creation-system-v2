Why Collagen Matters as We Age

Collagen is the body’s heavy-hitter when it comes to supporting skin elasticity and promoting healthy hair, nails, joints, and connective tissues. Naturally produced collagen begins to decline in your mid-20s, with noticeable physical changes occurring in the 30s and 40s, including the appearance of wrinkles or fine lines, sagging and dry skin, and internal changes such as joint stiffness or slower cell renewal. Because collagen is so central to visible and non-visible signs of aging, high-quality formulas like Collagène can provide a proactive advantage when it comes to supporting a healthy aging process.

How to Choose the Right Collagen Supplement

When choosing a collagen supplement, it may surprise you to realize that how much collagen a product contains has little to do with its efficacy–or the body’s ability to actually use it. Hydrolyzed collage, also known as collagen peptides, is broken down into smaller chains that are easier for the body to absorb. In addition to the size and bioavailability, the source also matters: marine collagen (from fish) is comprised mainly of type I collagen which is prized for its smaller peptide size and quick absorption, ability to support the health of the skin, bones, tendons, ligaments, and teeth, and it’s skin-focused benefits like appearance of skin firmness, elasticity and the visual reduction of fine lines. In contrast, Bovine collagen (sourced from cows) naturally contains both Type 1 and Type III collagen. Type III collagen is more prevalent in skin, muscles, blood vessels, and internal organs. It contributes to the appearance of skin elasticity and hydration, and helps support the structure of connective tissue.

Regardless of where you source your collagen from, the most important factor is absorption, or in other words, bioavailability. Without good absorption, much of the collagen you consume simply won’t reach the tissues where it’s needed the most.
