Think of vitamin C as the multitasking hero of skincare: it supports, enhances, and nourishes all at once.

As a potent antioxidant, vitamin C supports your skin's natural reaction to free radicals—unstable molecules caused by UV rays and pollution—that stress and dull the look of the skin. At the cellular level, vitamin C visually brightens and helps reduce the appearance of dark spots and uneven skin tone, making the skin more even and radiant.

It also supports the body's natural production of collagen to enhance skin elasticity, bounce, and glow. Vitamin C is not just a nice-to-have ingredient—it's an essential for long-term skin health and foundational to any skincare routine.

How to Use Vitamin C Serum for Maximum Results

To get the most out of your vitamin C serum, apply it in the morning on clean, dry skin—just after cleansing and toning and before moisturizer and sunscreen. It is best to use vitamin C in the morning, rather than at night, so that it can combine with sunscreen to boost your skin's defense against UV rays and photoaging.

Use a pea-sized amount and gently press it into your face and neck with your fingers. Remember—more is not always better. Overuse can cause sensitivity, so start slow and don’t overdo it. If you have sensitive skin, start by applying vitamin C a few times a week and slowly work up to daily use.

Vitamin C can be used with hydrating ingredients like hyaluronic acid and niacinamide, but avoid pairing it with any acids or exfoliants. If you’re using retinol or exfoliating acids in your skincare routine, use them at night to avoid irritation. Consistent use can lead to visibly brighter and more even skin tone in 2-4 weeks, with improvements in the look of firmness, texture, and fine lines. Be mindful of oxidation: if your serum darkens significantly, it may have degraded and lost effectiveness.
