Why Cellular Absorption Matters for Metabolic Supplements

Cellular absorption refers to how quickly and effectively nutrients move through your digestive tract, enter the bloodstream, and pass into your cells, where they are used to support cellular functions.

In case you missed it, that last step (crossing the cellular membrane) is really important! No matter how powerful or pure the ingredient is on paper, if the nutrients in your supplement can’t cross the cell membrane, it’s sure to impact its efficacy. The ease with which a nutrient crosses the cellular membrane is referred to as bioavailability–or in other words, how likely it is that a nutrient can be absorbed and used by your cells once it has entered the bloodstream. Because cellular absorption impacts efficacy, bioavailability has become a major focus in the supplement market.

Because we want to encourage high bioavailability, our advanced absorption technology is designed to:

- Stabilize sensitive compounds from being broken down during digestion

- Support transport across the intestinal barrier and into the bloodstream

- Improve cellular uptake

Advanced absorption technology is especially important for ingredients like peptides and botanical extracts, which are notoriously difficult to deliver effectively. Peptides can break down in the digestive tract and lose effectiveness before reaching cells if they lack the right delivery technology to survive digestion and facilitate transport across the intestinal barrier.
