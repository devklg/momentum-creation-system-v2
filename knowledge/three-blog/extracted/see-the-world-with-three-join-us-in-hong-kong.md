What Should You Know Before Visiting Hong Kong?

Hong Kong is a bustling metropolis filled with rich history, modern architecture, and world-renowned cuisine. Whether you’re visiting for the first time or returning for another adventure, here’s what you need to know before your trip:

Travel Tips for First-Time Visitors

- Currency & Payments: Hong Kong uses the Hong Kong Dollar (HKD). Credit cards are widely accepted, but it’s always good to have some cash on hand for street markets and local shops.

- Language: While Cantonese is the primary language, English is widely spoken, especially in hotels, restaurants, and major attractions.

- Transportation: The MTR (subway) system is the fastest way to get around, and taxis are also readily available.

- Weather in March: Expect comfortable temperatures between 16°C-22°C (61°F-72°F), making it the perfect time for sightseeing.

- Packing Essentials: Bring comfortable walking shoes, a light jacket, and an umbrella—Hong Kong’s weather can change quickly!

- Cultural Etiquette: Tipping is not expected in restaurants, but it’s appreciated for excellent service. Also, avoid sticking chopsticks upright in rice, as it symbolizes bad luck in Chinese culture.

For more travel planning, check out the Hong Kong Tourism Board for helpful resources on attractions, transportation, and dining.

Why Hong Kong? A Destination Worth Exploring

Hong Kong is more than just a business hub—it’s a cultural and culinary paradise. Whether you love cityscapes, nature, or food, there’s something for everyone.

Must-See Attractions in Hong Kong

- Victoria Peak – Ride the historic Peak Tram to enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of the city skyline.

- Temple Street Night Market – Experience Hong Kong’s vibrant street food scene, live entertainment, and local vendors.

- Lantau Island & Big Buddha – Visit this iconic 112-foot statue, take a scenic cable car ride, and explore Po Lin Monastery.

- Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade – Walk along the harbor and watch the city light up at night. Don’t miss the Symphony of Lights show!

Thinking about extending your stay? Hong Kong is the perfect gateway to explore more of Asia—consider a short trip to Macau, Thailand, or Japan after the convention!
