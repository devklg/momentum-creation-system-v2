How Your Natural Detox Network Works

- Liver (Phase I & II) – Converts fat-soluble compounds into water-soluble forms for elimination.

- Kidneys – Filter roughly 150 quarts of blood daily, sending water-soluble waste to your bladder.

- Lymphatic system – Drains cellular debris and immune by-products, but relies on daily movement to stay active.

- Gut & Microbiome – Bind heavy metals, create bulk for stool, and decide whether toxins get excreted or re-absorbed.

- Skin & Lungs – Secondary exits for minor waste through sweat and breath.

Modern foods stripped of minerals, combined with environmental stressors and sedentary habits, can slow this network down. Targeted nutritional support helps each pathway run the way nature intended.
