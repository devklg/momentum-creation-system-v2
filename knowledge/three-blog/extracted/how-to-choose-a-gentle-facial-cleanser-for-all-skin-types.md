What Makes a Facial Cleanser Gentle

Cleanser is meant to remove dirt, oil, makeup, and impurities from your skin—but if the formula is too harsh, it can do more harm than good. Just because a product is labeled “gentle” or “hydrating,” doesn’t mean it actually is. In skincare, “gentle” means:

- pH-balanced to match your skin’s natural acidity

- Non-stripping to avoid damaging skin barrier

- Non-irritating for calmer looking

- Fragrance-free or lightly scented with natural botanicals

- Formulated simply without unnecessary additives

When we say Pure Cleanse is gentle, we mean it. This luxury facial cleanser is made with high-quality, natural ingredients that cleanse effectively while hydrating and nourishing the skin, leaving it prepped for the rest of your routine.

Facial Cleanser Ingredients to Look For and Avoid

When looking for a gentle facial cleanser, it's important to read the ingredient list and see what’s actually inside the bottle. Here’s what to look for—and what you should leave on the shelf.

Ingredients to look for:

- Glycerine: a humectant that attracts and retains moisture

- Hyaluronic acid: visually plumps and hydrates the skin

- Aloe vera: naturally soothes and calms the skin

- Ceramides: help support the skin barrier

Ingredients to avoid:

- Fragrance/parfum: a common trigger for sensitive skin

- Sulfates: harsh detergents that can strip natural oils

- Alcohol: dehydrates the skin
