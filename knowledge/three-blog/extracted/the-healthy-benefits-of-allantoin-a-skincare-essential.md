What is Allantoin?

Allantoin is a naturally occurring compound found in plants such as comfrey, chamomile, and, as we’ll discuss later, in many plants less traditionally associated with health like the desert plantain.

It’s also a compound our bodies produce, which contributes to its gentle nature. Allantoin is celebrated in skincare specifically for its ability to soothe, and hydrate—all while being kind to even the most sensitive complexions.

Why Your Skin Loves Allantoin

- Moisturization Allantoin helps skin retain moisture by forming a barrier that locks in hydration. This helps maintain a plump, youthful appearance.

- Cell Renewal One of allantoin’s most notable benefits is its ability to promote the skin’s natural exfoliation process, which encourages the shedding of dead skin cells and the appearance of fresher, more radiant skin.

- Skin Soothing Allantoin is known for its calming properties, which can help reduce the appearance of redness and soothe skin discomfort, making it ideal for those with sensitive skin.










 "Think of allantoin as your skin’s best friend—always there to hydrate and soothe."







Allantoin's benefits extend beyond basic skincare. Here's why it's worth incorporating into your routine:

- It’s incredibly gentle on skin. Its non-irritating nature makes it suitable for all skin types, including sensitive skin.

- It can help visually improve skin texture. Regular use of products containing allantoin can lead to the appearance of smoother, more refined skin texture.
