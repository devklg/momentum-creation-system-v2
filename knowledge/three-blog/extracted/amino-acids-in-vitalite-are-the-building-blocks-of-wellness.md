The Basics of Amino Acids

Before we dive into the specific amino acids included in Vitalité and their benefits, let's start with the basics. Amino acids are organic compounds that serve as the building blocks of proteins, which are essential for the health of tissues and organs in the body. There are 20 standard amino acids, each with its unique chemical structure and role.

These amino acids can be classified into two categories: essential and non-essential. Essential amino acids cannot be synthesized by the body and must be obtained through diet or supplementation. Non-essential amino acids, on the other hand, can be produced by the body itself, although they may also be obtained from dietary sources.

The Role of Amino Acids in Health and Wellness

Amino acids play a crucial role in various physiological processes, including:

- Protein Synthesis: Amino acids are the building blocks of proteins, which are essential for the maintenance of tissues in the body. Protein synthesis is necessary for muscle development, immune response, and enzyme activity.

- Neurotransmitter Production: Certain amino acids serve as precursors for neurotransmitters, which are chemical messengers that facilitate communication between nerve cells in the brain and nervous system.

- Energy Production: Amino acids can be metabolized to produce energy when carbohydrates and fats are not available. This process, known as gluconeogenesis, involves converting amino acids into glucose to fuel various metabolic processes in the body.

- Antioxidant Activity: Some amino acids, such as cysteine and glycine, are components of antioxidant enzymes that help provide support to cells from oxidative stress caused by free radicals. By neutralizing free radicals, these amino acids contribute to overall health and longevity.

- Immune Response: Amino acids play a vital role in supporting immune response by aiding in the production of antibodies, cytokines, and other immune system components.
