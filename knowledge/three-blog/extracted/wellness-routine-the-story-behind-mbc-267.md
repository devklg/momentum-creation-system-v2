A Discovery Born in The Norwegian Sea

The Norwegian Sea is one of the most untouched marine environments on Earth. Fed by cold Arctic currents and shielded from the kind of industrial pollution that affects lesser habitats, it has long been considered a gold standard for purity. The salmon that thrive in these waters are themselves a testament to that environment: resilient, clean, and biologically remarkable.

It was within this ecosystem that researchers identified something extraordinary: a naturally occurring peptide complex with unique properties related to metabolic support. This complex, found in Norwegian salmon as well as in certain mushrooms, became the foundation for what THREE International would develop in MBC-267.

It’s important to note what MBC-267 is not. The peptides in MBC-267 from salmon are completely different in structure and in function from omega-3 fatty acids like EPA and DHA. Fish oil works through fatty acid pathways. MBC-267 peptides are a distinctive class of bioactive molecules (proteins broken down into smaller chains) that support the body’s systems in an entirely different way. Calling them the same thing would be like confusing silk with cotton because both come from nature.

What makes these among the best peptides for supporting metabolic wellness is precisely their origin: they were shaped by millions of years of natural biology in one of the world’s most demanding environments, not engineered to mimic something else.

Where Nature Meets Biotechnology

Discovering MBC-267 was only half the challenge. Nature had already done the remarkable work of creating these peptides; THREE International’s task was figuring out how to get them where they needed to go inside the human body.

Peptides, by their very nature, are vulnerable. When ingested, the digestive system is efficient at breaking down proteins and peptide chains before they can reach their destination. For the benefits of MBC-267 to be realized, it needed a delivery system sophisticated enough to match the ingredient itself.

THREE developed a proprietary cellular absorption technology that does exactly that. Here’s how it works: polyphenols are used to coat and surround the MBC-267 peptide complex. This natural wrapping acts as a kind of shield, allowing the peptide complex to pass through the GI tract without breaking down prematurely.

Once MBC-267 has safely navigated the GI tract, adjuvant technology takes over. This mechanism helps the peptide complex diffuse through the cellular membrane, where it can exert its beneficial biological activity at the cellular level. The result is a delivery pathway from source to cell, intact and functional.
