The world of skincare can be intimidating.

There are countless lotions, potions, and creams on the market, as well as skincare serums. And with so many options, how do you know which products to choose? Do you really need a serum?

Don’t worry, we’re here to help you demystify the world of skincare! We’ll show you the benefits of using a face serum as part of your daily routine and recommend the best serums for wrinkles and fine lines.

7 Signs You Need a Skincare Serum

Curious about how to know if you need a serum for your face? Odds are, your skin is already telling you everything you need to know. Here are seven signs your skin needs a serum:

Dryness

If your skin is dry and you feel like lotion isn’t working anymore, a skincare serum may be what you need to provide hydration in a hurry. Serums can be more effective than lotions because they have higher concentrations of active ingredients. This, combined with the smaller molecule size of serums, makes it easier for your skin to absorb, which means it works faster and is more effective than a lotion or cream.

Uneven Tone

You can use a serum for dry skin AND uneven tone. Dry patches, uneven melanin production, dead skin buildup, and fine lines can all contribute to uneven skin tone. A skincare serum can help brighten the complexion and improve the appearance and texture of the skin, which in turn leads to a smoother, more even-looking tone.

Rough Texture

Rough texture of the skin can be caused by many factors, including dryness, fine lines and wrinkles, dead skin buildup, and clogged pores. Yikes! A skincare serum helps moisturize and exfoliate your face to provide a smoother-looking surface.

Wrinkles

Fine lines and wrinkles are a part of life, but that doesn’t mean you have to take them as they come, because another one of the benefits of using a face serum is its ability to smooth the appearance of wrinkles. Serums do this in several ways. First, they moisturize the skin, then they support collagen production, both of which help reduce the appearance of wrinkles.

Large Pores

Many factors, including genetics, sun damage, aging, makeup, excess oil production, and more, can contribute to large facial pores.

While a skincare serum can’t change your genetics, it can unclog, exfoliate, and refine the look of your skin, as well as help manage oil production, which will leave your pores looking fine. (Literally.)

Dullness

Even if your skin isn’t particularly dry, it can still be dull.

Your skin is in a constant state of shedding, with a complete skin turnover happening every 28-30 days. Sometimes the body needs a little help getting rid of that old skin. Buildup of skin cells can cause your face to appear dull. A skincare serum can help exfoliate the dead skin and brighten up your new skin for a radiant look.

Dark Spots

Dark spots appear as a result of uneven melanin production. A facial serum can help reduce the appearance of existing dark spots and circles.

How to Choose the Right Skincare Serum for Your Needs

Now that you know the importance of a skincare serum, how do you know which one to choose?

Here’s a hint: Not all skincare serums are created equal. There are a few benefits you should look for when selecting your next skincare serum.

Ingredient Transparency

A skincare company should be able to tell you what ingredients are in the product, where they came from, and how the product is produced. The people demand cleaner skincare products, and with good reason! Your body is absorbing your skincare serum, so make sure you know what’s in it and what it’s doing to (and for) your skin.

Clinical Backing

While science plays a part in most skincare development, not all skincare is clinically backed. Clinical backing means there is scientific research and evidence of effectiveness. A clinically backed product also tends to be deemed safer, and has usually undergone clinical trials with testing on humans. The studies help validate the products and attest to their benefits.

Neurocosmetics

There’s a link between the way you look and the way you feel. Neurocosmetics are strengthening that link. Neurocosmetic skincare contains bioactive ingredients that interact with the body’s nervous system to help you feel good from the inside out.

Ease of Use

Let’s face it, the more complicated your skincare routine gets, the less likely you are to stick with it. A skincare serum that helps simplify your daily routine is an added bonus!
