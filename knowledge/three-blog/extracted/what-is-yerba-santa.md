What Is Yerba Santa and Where Does It Come From?

Yerba Santa (eriodictyon californicum) is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to the southwestern regions of the United States. Primarily found in California, this shrub is rich in flavonoids like eriodictyol and resins that are rich in bioactive compounds that are known to support a healthy inflammatory response. Long known for its ability to support respiratory health, Indigenous tribes and early settlers used this “sacred herb” for various uses.More recently, Yerba Santa has been recognized for its connection to energy support. Unlike Yerba Mate, which is a natural source of caffeine, Yerba Santa does not contain caffeine. However, specialty molecules found in the Yerba Santa plant support healthy adrenal function and the body’s stress response.In other words, Yerba Santa helps support a more balanced energy release, keeping the dreaded caffeine crash at bay.

Benefits of Yerba Santa: From Energy to Adrenal Support

In addition to its traditional uses, Yerba Santa has a number of unique benefits that make it an intriguing addition to natural energy support. Yerba Santa also releases resin that is often rich with antioxidants, which may help support the body’s own inflammatory responses.Recent research has identified a high concentration of sterubin, a neurosupportive flavonoid that helps support cognitive function and supports a healthy inflammatory response. Additionally, sterubin may contribute to balanced energy production at the cellular level.
