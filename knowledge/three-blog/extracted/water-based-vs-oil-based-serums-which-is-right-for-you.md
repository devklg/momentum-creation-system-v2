When it comes to serums, one of the most important choices you’ll make is between a water-based or oil-based formula. Each type has its benefits, but understanding the differences will help you select the serum that best suits your skin’s needs.

In this article, we’ll explore the key distinctions between water-based and oil-based serums and give you the behind-the-scenes look at why Visage Super Serum is water-based.

What makes a water-based serum?

Water-based serums are (surprise!) usually formulated with water as their primary ingredient, making them light, fast-absorbing, and highly effective at delivering active ingredients deep into the skin. These serums are ideal when you want a lightweight feel and/or are looking to layer multiple products in your skincare routine.

The benefits of water-based serums

- They’re lightweight and fast absorbing! As we explained previously, water-based serums are known for their light texture, which allows your skin to absorb them quickly, without leaving residue. This makes them an excellent choice for those with oily or combination skin, or for anyone who prefers a non-greasy finish.

- They let ingredients penetrate more deeply into your skin. Because water is a universal solvent, it’s excellent at dissolving active ingredients and helping them penetrate the skin’s surface. This means that the active ingredients in water-based serums can work more effectively, reaching the deeper layers of the skin where they’re needed most!

- They play well with others. If you enjoy a multi-step skincare routine, water-based serums are perfect for layering. Their lightweight texture allows them to be applied under other products without feeling heavy or clogging pores (which means you still get all the benefits from the rest of your routine).





 Water-based serums offer a lightweight, fast-absorbing solution that delivers active ingredients deep into the skin, making them an essential part of any skincare routine.







What makes an oil-based serum?

These serums are made with oils as their primary ingredient, giving them a richer, more nourishing feel. There’s no doubt that oil-based serums are having a moment in the skincare world. Their rich texture and deep hydration make them particularly appealing to those looking for indulgent skincare products. However, while they may be trendy, oil-based serums can sometimes struggle to mix well with other products, which might limit their effectiveness, especially in a layered skincare routine.

The benefits of oil-based serums

- They can really “lock in” moisture. Oil-based serums provide a rich layer of hydration that’s particularly beneficial for dry or dehydrated skin. The oils in these serums help to lock in moisture and create a protective barrier on the skin’s surface. They can, however, potentially lead to clogged pores or a greasy finish.

- They’re champs at providing nourishment. Many oils are packed with vitamins and fatty acids that can help nourish your skin and soothe irritation or dryness. They can also help to smooth the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

- They can stay on your skin longer. Because of their rich texture, oil-based serums tend to stick around longer on the skin. This can also mean that it takes a longer time for your skin to absorb all the goodness contained within the serum, which risks the active ingredients being accidentally wiped off or otherwise removed before you can take full advantage.
